SECTION 1: SYSTEM OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The Laseronics LX-5000 is a 6kW fiber laser cutting system designed for
high-throughput sheet metal fabrication. It supports material thicknesses from
0.5mm to 30mm (mild steel), 0.5mm to 25mm (stainless steel), and 0.5mm to
20mm (aluminum alloy). The cutting envelope measures 3000mm x 1500mm with a
positional accuracy of ±0.03mm.

Primary Components:
- IPG Photonics YLS-6000 fiber laser source (6kW rated output)
- Precitec ProCutter 2.0 cutting head with integrated capacitive height control
- Bosch Rexroth linear drive system (X, Y, Z axes)
- Siemens 840D sl CNC controller
- Automatic pallet changer (2-pallet shuttle system)
- Exhaust and filtration unit: Kemper PowerMaster 4000

SECTION 2: PREVENTIVE MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
================================================================================

2.1 DAILY MAINTENANCE (Every 8-Hour Shift)

Before each production shift, the operator must complete the following:

1. Inspect the cutting head nozzle for spatter buildup or deformation.
   Replace the nozzle if the orifice diameter deviates more than 0.1mm from
   nominal. Nozzle part numbers: D1.0 (P/N: LX-NOZ-10), D1.5 (P/N: LX-NOZ-15),
   D2.0 (P/N: LX-NOZ-20).

2. Clean the protective glass (cover slide) in the cutting head.
   Procedure:
   a. Power down beam emission (turn key switch to SAFE position).
   b. Remove the slide cartridge by pressing the release tab on the left side
      of the head housing.
   c. Inspect glass for burns, cracks, or heavy spatter. Replace if any
      damage is visible (P/N: LX-GLASS-22).
   d. Wipe clean with optical-grade IPA wipe (single-pass, one direction).
   e. Reinstall cartridge until audible click confirms lock.

3. Verify assist gas pressures at the manifold:
   - Nitrogen: 20 bar (±0.5 bar)
   - Oxygen: 6 bar (±0.2 bar)
   - Air assist: 8 bar (±0.3 bar)

4. Check the chiller unit (SMC Thermo-Chiller). Coolant temperature should
   read 22°C ±1°C. Top up coolant (distilled water + 5% inhibitor) if level
   is below the MIN mark.

5. Confirm extraction unit is running and differential pressure across the
   filter cartridges is below 1200 Pa. Replace filter cartridge set if
   pressure exceeds 1500 Pa (P/N: KEM-FILT-4000).

2.2 WEEKLY MAINTENANCE (Every 40 Operating Hours)

1. Lubricate X and Y axis linear guides using an automatic lubrication
   dispenser (Bijur Delimon DL4) or manual application of Klüber Isoflex NBU 15.
   Apply 2-3ml per lubrication point. Reference lubrication map: Drawing LX5000-LUB-01.

2. Inspect the beam delivery fiber cable for kinks, sharp bends, or connector
   contamination. The minimum bend radius is 200mm. Never bend the fiber cable
   below this limit. Clean the QD fiber connector with a lint-free swab
   dampened with acetone before inspecting under a fiber scope.

3. Clean the scrap conveyor belt and remove accumulated slag from the cutting
   table slats. Worn or warped slats must be replaced to maintain workpiece
   flatness (P/N: LX-SLAT-60).

4. Verify home position accuracy by running the Precision Check Routine (PCR)
   from the Siemens controller:
   Navigate to: Menu > Diagnostics > PCR > Run Full Cycle
   Acceptable result: Position error < ±0.05mm on all axes.

2.3 MONTHLY MAINTENANCE (Every 160 Operating Hours)

1. Replace the cutting head nozzle regardless of visual condition.

2. Inspect and clean the beam collimator lens inside the cutting head using
   a specialized cleaning kit (P/N: LX-OPTKIT-01). This procedure must be
   performed by a certified Laseronics technician or trained maintenance
   engineer. Improper handling can permanently damage the optical assembly.

3. Check the laser power output calibration:
   a. Install the calibrated power meter (Ophir Nova II with FL250A-BB-50 head)
      at the cutting head position.
   b. Set laser to 10% power (600W), CW mode, no motion.
   c. Measure output for 30 seconds and record average.
   d. Acceptable range: 570W–630W. If outside range, contact Laseronics
      service (service@laseronics.com) to schedule calibration.

4. Drain and replace chiller coolant. Flush circuit with fresh distilled water
   before refilling. Coolant specification: 95% deionized water + 5%
   Antifrogen N inhibitor.

5. Inspect Z-axis bellows for cracks or holes. Replace if deteriorated
   (P/N: LX-BELLOW-Z).

SECTION 3: ERROR CODES AND TROUBLESHOOTING
================================================================================

3.1 COMMON ALARM CODES

ERR-101: LASER SOURCE INTERLOCK OPEN
  Cause: Safety interlock on laser source chassis triggered.
  Action: Check the red FAULT indicator on the IPG laser rack. Open the
  rear panel and verify all interlock connectors are fully seated. Reset
  by pressing RESET on the laser source HMI. If fault persists, check
  the interlock chain for damaged wiring (refer to wiring diagram LX5000-WD-07).

ERR-102: CHILLER TEMPERATURE OUT OF RANGE
  Cause: Coolant temperature exceeded 25°C or fell below 18°C.
  Action: Check ambient temperature in the machine room (must be 18–28°C).
  Verify chiller setpoint is 22°C. Check coolant level and inspect
  chiller pump operation. Allow system to stabilize for 10 minutes before
  attempting restart.

ERR-103: ASSIST GAS PRESSURE LOW
  Cause: Line pressure below threshold for selected gas circuit.
  Action: Check the manifold gauge for the active gas. Inspect supply
  cylinder or plant line pressure. Check for leaks at the solenoid valve
  block (listen for hissing). Verify hose fittings are not loose.

ERR-201: CAPACITIVE SENSOR FAULT
  Cause: Cutting head height sensor is reporting an implausible value.
  Action: 1) Clean the nozzle and check for metallic spatter bridging
  the nozzle body. 2) Check the sensor cable connector at the cutting head.
  3) Jog Z axis manually and observe sensor readout in Diagnostics > I/O Monitor.
  Expected output: 0–10V linear with distance. If reading is constant or
  zero, replace the sensor (P/N: LX-CAPSENS-01).

ERR-202: FOLLOW ERROR EXCEEDED — AXIS X/Y/Z
  Cause: Servo position error exceeded the configured tolerance (typically
  set to 2mm). Common causes: mechanical obstruction, servo drive fault,
  encoder cable failure.
  Action: Clear the alarm, then jog the affected axis slowly. Listen for
  grinding or irregular motion. Check the Siemens drive status under:
  Menu > Diagnostics > Drive Status. A drive fault code will be displayed.
  Refer to Siemens 840D Diagnostics Manual (DOC SIE-840D-DIAG) for drive
  fault resolution.

ERR-305: FILTER UNIT DIFFERENTIAL PRESSURE HIGH
  Cause: Extraction filter cartridges are saturated or the extraction fan
  has failed.
  Action: Check filter differential pressure reading on the Kemper unit
  display. If > 1500 Pa, replace filter set. If pressure is high even
  with new filters, inspect the extraction fan impeller for debris buildup.

ERR-401: PART PROGRAM FILE NOT FOUND
  Cause: CNC attempted to load a part program that does not exist at the
  specified path.
  Action: Verify the file name and directory on the Siemens PCU. Programs
  must be stored in /NC_DATA/MPF/ for main programs or /NC_DATA/SPF/ for
  subroutines. Transfer the program from CAM station via DNC link and retry.

ERR-500: EMERGENCY STOP ACTIVE
  Cause: E-stop button pressed, safety fence gate opened, or light curtain
  interrupted.
  Action: Identify and address the safety condition. Physically inspect
  the machine perimeter. Reset the E-stop mushroom button (rotate and pull).
  Press RESET on the operator panel and then CYCLE START only when it is
  safe to do so.

4.3 FOCUS POSITION

The focal point position is measured relative to the top surface of the material:

- Mild steel (O2): focus at +1mm to +3mm above the surface
- Stainless steel (N2): focus at -1mm to -2mm (inside the material)
- Aluminum: focus at -2mm to -4mm

Adjust focus using the F_POS parameter in the technology table or via:
Operator Panel > Technology > Focus Offset.

SECTION 5: CALIBRATION PROCEDURES
================================================================================

5.1 NOZZLE ALIGNMENT CALIBRATION

Performed after every nozzle replacement and whenever cut quality degrades.

1. Install a new or clean nozzle of appropriate diameter.
2. From the Siemens panel, navigate to: Technology > Calibration > Nozzle Align.
3. The machine will automatically fire a low-power pulse at a calibration
   target plate and calculate the beam-to-nozzle offset.
4. If the offset exceeds ±0.15mm, the system will prompt to adjust the
   nozzle holder via two set-screws (Allen key 2.5mm). Adjust incrementally
   and repeat until offset is within tolerance.
5. Record the calibration result in the Maintenance Log (Form LX-ML-01).

5.2 WORKPIECE ZERO CALIBRATION

Each job requires setting the sheet material origin:
1. Load the material onto the cutting table and clamp.
2. On the Siemens panel: Workpiece > Set Reference Point.
3. Jog the cutting head to the corner of the sheet.
4. Press SET ZERO to define X=0, Y=0.
5. Z zero is set automatically by the capacitive height sensor during
   the first rapid traverse over the sheet edge.

5.3 POWER CALIBRATION LOG

All power calibration events must be logged. Required fields:
- Date and time
- Technician name and certification number
- Measured power at 10%, 50%, and 100% commanded output
- Pass/Fail result
- Next calibration due date (3 months from current)

SECTION 6: SAFETY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

WARNING: The LX-5000 is a Class 4 laser product. Direct or indirect exposure
to the laser beam can cause immediate and permanent eye injury and severe skin
burns. Always ensure all safety interlocks are functional before operation.

Required Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):
- Laser safety glasses rated for 1070nm (OD 5+ for maintenance, OD 3+ for operation
  behind interlocked enclosure)
- Heat-resistant gloves when handling freshly cut parts
- Respiratory protection (N95 minimum) when cleaning residues without extraction

Safety Interlock Tests (perform quarterly):
1. Open the access door while the laser is enabled — confirm ERR-500 triggers.
2. Interrupt the light curtain at the pallet changer — confirm machine stops.
3. Press each E-stop button individually — confirm all axes halt within 200ms.

Emergency Procedures:
- In case of fire: Activate nearest pull station, press all E-stops, evacuate.
  Do not use water on electrical fires. CO2 or dry chemical extinguisher only.
- In case of fume release: Evacuate immediately, ventilate the area, do not
  re-enter until industrial hygienist clears the space.
- Laser exposure incident: Seek immediate medical attention. Log the incident
  per OSHA 300 requirements.
